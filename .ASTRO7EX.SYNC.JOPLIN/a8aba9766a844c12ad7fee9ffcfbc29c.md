## Purpose of Layer 0 Components

Layer 0 components enumerate **every axis along which reality can constrain narrative**.

They exist to:
- Eliminate hidden rule creation
- Prevent late-stage exceptions
- Make reality testable and auditable
- Anchor all higher layers in non-negotiable limits

If a constraint affects **existence, possibility, or irreversibility**, it belongs in Layer 0.

---

## What Counts as a Layer 0 Component

A component belongs to Layer 0 if it satisfies **all three** conditions:

1. It is true regardless of observers  
2. It applies before, during, and after the story  
3. It cannot be overridden by theme, character, or plot  

If a rule can be bent for drama, it is **not Layer 0**.

## 0.1 Existence & Ontology

**Concern:** What exists and what does not.

- **Entity Existence**
  - What kinds of entities can exist (material, abstract, informational)
- **Ontological Categories**
  - Distinction between physical, mental, symbolic, systemic
- **Boundary of Reality**
  - What is real vs illusory vs imagined
- **Continuity of Existence**
  - Whether entities persist when unobserved
- **Substrate Dependence**
  - Whether existence requires a medium (matter, energy, data)
- **Uniqueness vs Multiplicity**
  - Whether identical entities can coexist
- **Identity Persistence**
  - What makes something the â€œsame thingâ€ over time

## 0.2 Truth Structure

**Concern:** Whether truth exists independently of belief.

- **Objective Truth**
  - Facts that exist regardless of perception
- **Subjective Experience**
  - Perception without truth authority
- **Knowability**
  - Whether truth can be discovered
- **Completeness of Knowledge**
  - Whether total knowledge is possible
- **Error Tolerance**
  - Degree to which perception deviates from reality
- **Truth Latency**
  - Delay between fact and discovery
- **Truth Decay**
  - Whether truth degrades over time

## 0.3 Temporal Mechanics

**Concern:** How time operates.

- **Directionality**
  - Forward-only, bidirectional, cyclical
- **Continuity**
  - Continuous vs discrete time
- **Reversibility**
  - Whether past states can be restored
- **Temporal Persistence**
  - Whether effects outlast causes
- **Concurrency**
  - Whether events can occur simultaneously
- **Temporal Scope**
  - Local vs global time effects
- **Memory vs Time**
  - Relationship between recollection and actuality

**Concern:** How effects arise.

## Component Interaction Rules

- Components **stack**, they do not replace each other  
- No component may contradict another  
- Higher-layer logic must obey all components simultaneously  
- Missing components default to **implicit assumptions** (dangerous)

---

## What Layer 0 Explicitly Excludes

Layer 0 does **not** include:
- Meaning or value judgments  
- Justice or fairness  
- Emotional truth  
- Narrative payoff  
- Character psychology  
- Audience expectation  

If it answers â€œwhy this matters,â€ it is **Layer 1+**, not Layer 0.

- Does decay require effort to prevent?
