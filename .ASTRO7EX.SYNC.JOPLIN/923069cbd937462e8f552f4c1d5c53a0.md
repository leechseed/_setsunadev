## Purpose of Ontological Layers

Ontological layers define **what kinds of truths exist in a story and which truths outrank others**.

They exist to:
- Prevent rule confusion  
- Stop character exceptionalism  
- Enforce thematic and systemic coherence  
- Provide a stable foundation for scaling  

If scaling answers *how far consequences spread*, ontology answers *what cannot change*.

---

## What â€œOntologicalâ€ Means in Narrative

In this framework, **ontology** refers to:

> The hierarchy of constraints that govern narrative reality, independent of presentation or taste.

Ontological layers answer questions like:
- What must always be true?
- What may change, and at what cost?
- What cannot be overridden by clever writing?

Ontological layers are **not story beats or techniques**.  
They are **structural facts about how the narrative universe operates**.

---

## The Ontological Layer Stack (Lowest â†’ Highest)

From most fundamental (least flexible) to most expressive (most flexible):

> **Reality â†’ Meaning â†’ Power â†’ Experience â†’ Expression â†’ Argument â†’ Perception**

Lower layers constrain higher layers.  
Higher layers may express or interpret lower layers, but never alter them.

## Layer-by-Layer Definitions

---

## Layer 0 â€” Ontological Reality
**(Hard constraint layer)**

### Definition  
The fundamental rules of existence in the story world.

### Governs
- Reality / ontology  
- Time  
- Causality  
- Permanence  
- Entropy / decay  
- Scale invariance  

### Core Question  
> *What is true even if no one is alive to witness it?*

### Invariants Belong Here
- Time does not reverse  
- Actions have irreversible consequences  
- Decay cannot be fully halted  

### Failure Mode
- Retcons  
- Exceptions for protagonists  
- World rules that change to save plot

---

## Layer 1 â€” Value & Meaning Logic
**(Moral constraint layer)**

### Definition  
The system by which actions gain or lose significance.

### Governs

- Moral logic

### Core Question

### Invariants Belong Here

- Power corrupts outcomes  
- Hope is not guaranteed  

### Failure Mode

- Moral reversals without consequence  
- Genre incoherence

## Rules of Interaction Between Layers

1. **Lower layers constrain higher layers**  
2. **Higher layers express lower layers**  
3. **No layer may override a lower layer**  
4. **Violations propagate upward and destabilize scale**

If a higher layer requires changing a lower one, the system is broken.

---

## Common Ontological Violations

- Character growth altering moral physics  
- Tone demanding rule exceptions  
- Structure retconning causality  
- Dialogue explaining away systemic limits  

Most narrative incoherence is ontological, not stylistic.

---

- Small scale activates higher layers only  
- Large scale activates deeper layers simultaneously  
- Scaling widens consequence radius without changing ontology
