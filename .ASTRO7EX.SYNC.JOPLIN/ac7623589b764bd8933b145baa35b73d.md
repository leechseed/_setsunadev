# EDIT THE SMALL STUFF — 10-Minute Micro-Edits

Use a focused ten-minute pass to clean clutter and tighten readability.

---

### **Micro-Edit Options**
1. **Rename minor characters by profession.**  
   *(e.g., “Receptionist” → “Front Desk Clerk,” “Orderly,” “Dock Foreman”)*

2. **Cut entrances and exits.**  
   *(Skip “walks in / leaves”; jump to action.)*

3. **Remove minor time-thieves** (receptionists, waiters, nurses, etc.) that don’t alter plot.  

4. **Delete segue crutches** like “that reminds me” / “speaking of,” **and** the dialogue that follows if it’s filler.  

5. **Trim dialogue hedges:** Cut “Yeah,” “So,” “Well” unless essential to voice or timing.  

6. **Reduce name overuse** for emphasis in dialogue.  
   *(“Listen, Jack—Jack…” → “Listen…”)*
   
7. **Cut verbal reactions to bad news** and let **visuals** carry impact.  

8. **Lose non-diegetic music cues** unless sourced on-screen.
