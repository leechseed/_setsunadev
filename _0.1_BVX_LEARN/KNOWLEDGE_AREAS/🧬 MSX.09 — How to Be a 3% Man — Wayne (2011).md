## CORE THESIS
Only about 3% of men intuitively understand women and attraction — but this understanding can be learned through consistent study and application of masculine polarity, challenge maintenance, and reading female behavior rather than chasing it.

---

## INVARIANTS
*These are the unchanging principles — true regardless of context.*

1. **Attraction grows in space.** Pursuing too aggressively kills attraction. Creating space — fewer texts, less availability, letting her come to you — allows desire to build.
2. **Women want to be with a man they had to earn.** Challenge is attractive. A man who is too easy, too available, and too eager to commit signals low value regardless of his actual qualities.
3. **Her interest level dictates the strategy.** When her interest is high, everything works. When it drops, no technique compensates. Read the level, adjust accordingly.
4. **Masculine-feminine polarity drives chemistry.** The man leads, decides, initiates. The woman responds, follows, and invests emotionally. Collapse the polarity and chemistry dies.
5. **Repetition is the method.** Wayne's signature instruction — read the book 10-15 times — reflects the principle that internalization requires repetition, not intellectual understanding.

## HEURISTICS
*Rules of thumb for real-time decision-making.*

| Situation | Do This | Not This |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| You want to ask her out | Set a definite date — time, place, activity — with confidence | Ask vague open-ended questions ("wanna hang out sometime?") |
| She doesn't respond to a text | Wait — silence is data; do not double-text or chase | Send follow-up messages, memes, or "just checking in" |
| The date is going well | Escalate physically and lead toward intimacy naturally | Play it safe, keep it friendly, and hope for a second date |
| She's pulling back or losing interest | Mirror her energy — pull back equally, give space | Increase effort, buy gifts, or over-communicate |
| She "tests" your confidence | Stay calm, amused, and unfazed — respond with humor, not defensiveness | Take the bait, argue, or explain yourself |
| You're in a new relationship | Maintain your hobbies, friends, and independence | Drop everything and revolve your schedule around her |
| You feel the urge to define the relationship early | Let her bring it up — the person who cares less about labels holds more power | Rush into exclusivity before she's demonstrated sustained high interest |

## CORE CONCEPTS

**1. The 3% Man**
Wayne's term for men who naturally understand attraction dynamics — they lead, they challenge, they don't chase. The other 97% over-pursue, over-communicate, and surrender their frame. The book is designed to bridge that gap through repeated study and behavioral correction.

**2. Hang Back Strategy**
The principle of letting her come to you. After initial approach and date-setting, reduce initiation. Let her text first, let her suggest plans, let her invest. The underlying logic: whoever invests more has less power, and women are most attracted when they feel they're pursuing.

**3. Passing Tests**
Women test men's confidence through provocations, mixed signals, and boundary-pushes. These are not conscious manipulations but instinctive assessments of strength. A man who passes — by remaining calm, amused, and non-reactive — increases her attraction. A man who fails — by getting defensive, upset, or needy — drops it.

**4. Ideal Woman Visualization**
Write a detailed description of your ideal partner — values, personality, appearance, lifestyle. This exercise clarifies what you actually want (most men have never done this) and prevents settling out of scarcity. It also creates a screening filter: instead of trying to attract everyone, you evaluate against your own standard.

**5. Lover, Not Friend**
Every interaction should frame you as a romantic-sexual prospect, never a platonic buddy. This means physical touch, flirting, and clear intent from the start. If she categorizes you as a friend early, the window rarely reopens.

## WHAT DOESN'T WORK
Over-texting, over-calling, being constantly available, buying affection with gifts or favors, pursuing a woman whose interest level has dropped, defining the relationship before she does, suppressing masculine leadership to appear "equal," applying rigid rules (like "never text first") without reading context, treating every woman identically regardless of her investment signals.
