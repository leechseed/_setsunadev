## 1. The Edge, Defined Precisely

The asset stack being mapped against every platform:

|Asset|Type|Where It's Load-Bearing|
|---|---|---|
|Adobe mastery (Premiere, After Effects)|Execution edge|Formats where production quality is the _visible_ bottleneck|
|Sound production / music|Execution edge|Audio-forward and high-polish formats|
|AI pipeline fluency (scripting, image gen, voice transform)|Throughput edge|Volume formats; iteration speed|
|Taste (military-utilitarian, brutalist, analog-cockpit aesthetics)|**Demand edge**|The only durable moat — everyone has tools, almost nobody has taste|
|Anonymity requirement / persona-layer comfort|Constraint + option|Eliminates face-forward formats; enables character-wrapped formats|

**Core principle carried through this report:** an execution edge only converts to revenue where the _median competitor's output is visibly worse than yours_. Where the bottleneck is personality, credibility, or access — the edge buys nothing.

## 2. Platform Economics Snapshot (June 2026)

The structural difference between the three platforms is _what actually pays_:

|Platform|Native payout reality|Real money source|Edge fit|
|---|---|---|---|
|**YouTube**|Long-form AdSense is the strongest native RPM of the three; genuinely livable at scale|Ad revenue (native) + sponsors|**Best** — long-form rewards craft|
|**TikTok**|Creator Rewards Program: ~$0.40–$1.00 RPM, requires 60s+ original video; high-CPM niches can exceed $1.00|TikTok Shop + brand deals; native payouts are supplementary|Medium — rewards hooks over polish|
|**Instagram**|Reels Play Bonus discontinued in the US; native payouts (Gifts, Subscriptions, Badges) are a supplement, not income|**Brand sponsorships dominate** — the vast majority of Reels creator income is brand deals|Weakest native fit — it's a brand-deal marketplace, not an ad-rev platform|

Two 2026-specific notes that matter:

- **TikTok stability is resolved.** The USDS joint venture (Oracle/Silver Lake/MGX majority) means US operation is no longer in question, and the algorithm has been reweighted toward US-centric content — a tailwind for English-language US creators.
- **All three platforms now penalize "AI slop."** TikTok's CRP explicitly disqualifies AI-generated content without human direction; YouTube suppresses mass-produced inauthentic content. This is a _moat subsidy_ for high-craft creators: the platforms are doing your competitive filtering for you.

## 4. Instagram — Possibility Map

**The platform's true nature first:** Instagram is not an ad-revenue platform for creators. Native payouts (Gifts, Subscriptions, Live Badges) are tip-jar money. Instagram is a **brand-deal marketplace and a storefront** — you build an audience there to sell sponsorships, products, or funnel traffic elsewhere. Every possibility below must be read through that lens: _the audience is the product; a brand or your own offer is the buyer._

This immediately reframes the edge question: on Instagram, your Adobe stack matters less for payouts and more for **follower acquisition velocity** — polished Reels out-compete in the feed, but the money event happens off-feed.

### Possibility Directions

**IG1 — Motion-design micro-explainers (Reels-native Y1/Y2 ports)** Cross-sections, "how it works" animations cut to 30–60s vertical. Demand: strong — engineering/military visual content performs in Reels. Monetization: weak natively; the play is audience aggregation → brand deals (tool brands, EDC/tactical brands, software) or funnel to YouTube. **Viability: medium-high as a _distribution arm_, low as a standalone business.**

**IG2 — Aesthetic curation/taxonomy account (the "taxonomic-aesthetic operator" made visible)** Curated + original content in your aesthetic lanes: brutalism, military-industrial design, analog cockpits, utilitarian fashion. Instagram is _the_ platform for aesthetic authority accounts. Monetization: brand partnerships (fashion/EDC/design brands pay well for aesthetic-niche accounts), affiliate, eventual own-product. Directly compounds the BVX brand thesis. **Viability: medium-high — best brand-deal fit of any IG direction, slowest to revenue.**

**IG3 — Carousel infographics (the DK idea, static)** The DK cross-section concept as carousel posts — labeled diagrams, visual taxonomies. Carousels still have the best save/share mechanics on the platform. Production cost per post is a fraction of video. Monetization: same brand-deal/funnel logic. **Viability: medium — cheapest test of the DK aesthetic anywhere; doubles as asset library for video versions.**

**IG4 — Waifu persona account** Character-fronted content on Instagram specifically. Weak fit: IG's persona economy is built on real faces and lifestyle; virtual-character accounts underperform vs. TikTok/YouTube equivalents. **Viability: low on this platform.**

**IG5 — Fragrance/EDC/wardrobe niche authority** Your actual consumer-taste domains (fragrance profiles, TAD-style utility wardrobe, gear). High brand-deal CPMs in fragrance and EDC specifically; affiliate economics are real. Requires some on-camera or at least hands/product presence — anonymity manageable. **Viability: medium — real money path, but it's a _taste influencer_ business, different muscle than motion design.**

**IG6 — Templates/assets storefront marketing** IG as the marketing surface for a Y8-style product business (AE templates, motion packs). The platform's storefront mechanics suit this. **Viability: medium-high _if_ Y8 exists; nothing without it.**

### Instagram structural verdict

No Instagram direction is a standalone business at your revenue bar. Its correct role in your system is one of three: **(a)** distribution arm for a YouTube channel, **(b)** brand-deal surface once an aesthetic-authority audience exists, or **(c)** storefront marketing for a product. Treating Instagram as a primary revenue platform is the most common strategic error in this space — the platform is structured so that money flows to creators _through_ it, not _from_ it.

## 5. TikTok — Possibility Map

**Platform nature:** TikTok in 2026 is the inverse of Instagram — native payouts now exist and are non-trivial (CRP at $0.40–$1.00 RPM, with high-CPM niches like tech/finance exceeding $1.00 and outliers reaching ~$2.50), but the requirements shape everything: **60+ second videos, original, human-directed, high completion rate.** Short clips and undirected AI content earn zero. TikTok Shop is where the largest creator incomes actually sit, and the US-content algorithm reweighting is a current tailwind.

The CRP rules are, almost word for word, a description of your edge: original, high-production-value, 60s+, completion-optimized content. The platform rewrote its payout rules to pay people like you and starve the slop farms.

### Possibility Directions

**TT1 — 60–90s engineering/military explainers (vertical Y1/Y2)** Cross-sections and "how it works" in CRP-qualifying length. Tech/engineering content sits in the higher RPM band. Completion rate is the metric — and tightly-paced motion design is a completion machine. **Viability: strong — the single best native-payout fit on TikTok, and every video is a YouTube Short + Reel for free.**

**TT2 — Waifu-fronted explainer persona** TikTok is the _correct_ platform for this concept — character accounts and VTuber-adjacent content are native to its culture in a way they aren't on IG. Voice-transformed persona + motion design + 60s+ format = CRP-eligible and differentiated. Risk: persona content can drift toward entertainment RPM bands (lower) rather than tech bands. **Viability: medium-high — highest-ceiling wildcard on this platform.**

**TT3 — Aesthetic/ambient vertical content** Brutalist environments, cockpit footage edits, sound-designed mood content. Performs in bursts, but low completion intent and weak CRP economics. **Viability: low-medium.**

**TT4 — TikTok Shop-anchored content** The real-money lane on TikTok — but it requires a product to sell. Without your own product, it means affiliate showcasing (EDC gear, tools, fragrance), which drags you toward face/hands lifestyle content. **Viability: medium _if_ a product exists (Y8 templates, eventual SlimeVR hardware, etc.); the Shop is the platform's actual profit center.**

**TT5 — Rapid-iteration concept sandbox** Not a business — a lab. TikTok's distribution gives new accounts real reach immediately (no subscriber cold-start like YouTube), making it the cheapest place on the internet to A/B test formats, hooks, personas, and topics before committing the YouTube channel identity. **Viability: high _as infrastructure_ — this is where the scrum instinct actually belongs.**

### TikTok structural verdict

TikTok is the only platform where your edge maps directly onto the native payout rubric, and the only one offering instant-distribution testing. But its RPM ceiling means it tops out as a **secondary income + primary laboratory**, not the main engine. The canonical 2026 pattern — build reach on TikTok, monetize deep on YouTube and owned products — is exactly the right shape for your stack.

## 6. Cross-Platform Structural Read

The three platforms aren't competing options — they're different organs:

|Role|Platform|Why|
|---|---|---|
|**Revenue engine**|YouTube long-form|Only native RPM that reaches the income bar; craft compounds|
|**Laboratory + reach**|TikTok|Instant distribution for testing; CRP pays for the experiments|
|**Brand surface + funnel**|Instagram|Brand-deal marketplace; aesthetic authority; storefront|

**The convergent finding:** every platform's strongest possibility is the _same content core_ — high-craft motion-design explainers in the military/industrial/mechanical lane (Y1/Y2 = TT1 = IG1/IG3). One production pipeline, three cuts: 8–15min YouTube master → 60–90s TikTok vertical → Reel + carousel. The DK aesthetic and optional waifu persona are wrappers on that core, not separate businesses.

The single highest-leverage structural fact: **a vertical-first or horizontal-first pipeline decision determines everything downstream.** Master-then-cut (YouTube-first) preserves the revenue engine; vertical-first optimizes the lab. This is the next decision gate, not a platform choice.

## 7. Knockouts — Eliminated by Reasoning, No Testing Needed

Killed across all platforms without spending a single production hour:

1. **Any face-forward format** — anonymity constraint; persona layer is the workaround where personality is needed.
2. **Pure information content with no visual moat** — AI slop floor + platform suppression; info is free, presentation isn't.
3. **News/trend reaction** — zero evergreen value; wastes the entire Adobe stack on 48-hour content.
4. **Instagram as primary revenue platform** — structurally a brand-deal marketplace; native payouts are tips.
5. **Sub-60s TikTok as a monetization strategy** — CRP-ineligible by rule; shorts are distribution, never revenue.
6. **Undirected AI-generated content at volume** — explicitly disqualified by TikTok CRP, suppressed by YouTube; the slop-farm era is policy-dead.
7. **Music as a revenue lane** — worst discovery economics on every platform; remains an IP/craft pursuit only.
8. **Niche-hopping on a single channel identity** — recommendation systems punish it; experiments go in the TikTok lab or a sandbox account.

## 8. Open Assumptions Still Requiring Testing

What reasoning _cannot_ resolve — the actual experiment backlog, in dependency order:

|#|Assumption|Cheapest test|
|---|---|---|
|A1|The DK-grade visual pipeline can be systematized to a sustainable per-video cost|Build 3 cross-section graphics end-to-end; time them|
|A2|The waifu persona adds retention rather than niche-collision drag|Same video, persona vs. clean VO, run both on TikTok lab account|
|A3|Vertical 60–90s explainers hit CRP completion thresholds|5-video TikTok batch; read completion rates|
|A4|Topic verticals within the military/industrial lane differ materially in demand|Hook/topic A-B testing in the lab account|
|A5|The master-then-cut pipeline is actually efficient (vs. native-vertical production)|Produce one piece both ways; compare hours|

A1 is the gating assumption for the entire structure — if DK-grade visuals can't be produced at sustainable cost, the whole craft-moat thesis collapses regardless of platform.
