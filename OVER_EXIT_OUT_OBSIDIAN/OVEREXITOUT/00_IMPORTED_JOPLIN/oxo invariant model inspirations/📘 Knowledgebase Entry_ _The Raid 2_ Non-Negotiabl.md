## Invariant Validation Across Scale

### Invariant 1: Violence Is Labor, Not Glory

- **Dialogue**  
  Violence is discussed pragmatically, never romantically.

- **Set-Pieces**  
  Fights emphasize fatigue, injury, and accumulation of damage.

- **Narrative Outcome**  
  Survival requires relentless exertion, not dominance.

âœ… Violence is work, not triumph.

- **Dialogue**  
  Corruption is treated as ambient and expected.

- **Character Arc**  
  Individual integrity fails to dismantle systemic rot.

- **Criminal Ecosystem**  
  Power circulates through police, gangs, and politicians alike.

âœ… Removing individuals does not cleanse the system.

- **Dialogue**  
  Promises are framed as obligations with consequences.

- **Character Arc**

  Betrayal and allegiance function as strategic tools.

âœ… Loyalty increases exposure, not safety.

### Invariant 4: There Is No Clean Exit

- **Dialogue**  
  Characters acknowledge that involvement marks them permanently.

- Singular villains as the source of corruption  
- Loyalty rewarded with safety or peace  
- Redemption through domination  
- Clean victory without residue
