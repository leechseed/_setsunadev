## Overview

This document defines the **Non-Negotiable Invariants** that enforce coherence across scale in *Blue Is the Warmest Color*.

This is not a romance about fulfillment.  
It is not a coming-out victory story.  
It is a structural account of **how intimacy exposes incompatibility, how desire outpaces identity, and how love does not neutralize class, ambition, or timing**.

These invariants remain true across:
- everyday conversations  
- sexual discovery  
- social and class friction  
- creative aspiration  
- the final emotional separation  

They are **constraints on meaning**, not aesthetic indulgences.

## Invariant Validation Across Scale

### Invariant 1: Desire Does Not Guarantee Alignment

- **Relational Arc**  
  Passion precedes shared direction.

- **Behavioral Pattern**  
  Wanting intensifies without resolving mismatch.

- **Narrative Outcome**  
  Love fails to integrate future paths.

**Validation**: Attraction is not architecture.

---

### Invariant 2: Intimacy Accelerates Difference

- **Domestic Rhythm**  
  Daily life exposes incompatible needs.

- **Social Circles**  
  Worlds overlap briefly, then repel.

- **Emotional Texture**  
  Closeness sharpens friction.

**Validation**: Proximity clarifies separation.

---

### Invariant 3: Identity Forms Under Social Pressure

- **Class Contrast**  
  Education, taste, and aspiration diverge.

- **Creative Ambition**  
  Growth in one partner displaces the other.

- **Structural Reality**  
  Love does not pause development.

**Validation**: Context shapes becoming.

---

### Invariant 4: Love Can Be Real and Still End

- **Final Encounters**  
  Affection persists without reconciliation.

- **Narrative Refusal**  
  No villain or betrayal resolves the break.

- **Emotional Truth**  
  Loss coexists with authenticity.

**Validation**: Ending is not negation.

## Failure Tests (What Dies)

The following are structurally disallowed:

- Passion framed as compatibility  
- Sex functioning as integration  
- Love neutralizing class difference  
- Growth portrayed as betrayal  
- Endings that retroactively invalidate intimacy  

If any appear, *Blue Is the Warmest Color* collapses into romantic myth.

---

## Operational Outcome

Because these invariants are enforced:

- Intimacy feels lived-in rather than idealized  
- Sex reads as connective but insufficient  
- Class and ambition remain structurally active  
- Separation feels tragic without moral blame  
- The ending resonates as honest rather than cruel
