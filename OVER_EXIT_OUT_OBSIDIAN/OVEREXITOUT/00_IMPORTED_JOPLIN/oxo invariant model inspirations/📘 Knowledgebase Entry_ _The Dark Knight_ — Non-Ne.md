## Overview

This document defines the **Non-Negotiable Invariants** that enforce coherence across scale in *The Dark Knight*.

This is not a superhero power fantasy.  
It is not a simple order-vs-chaos parable.  
It is a structural account of **why moral systems fracture under pressure and why victory requires becoming something unbearable**.

These invariants remain true across:
- interrogations and courtroom rhetoric  
- public spectacle and private sacrifice  
- vigilantism and institutional response  
- escalating ethical dilemmas  
- the final narrative inversion  

They are **constraints on meaning**, not genre conventions.

## Failure Tests (What Dies)

The following are structurally disallowed:

- Villainy as madness alone  
- Institutions saving the day intact  
- Heroism without ethical compromise  
- Truth functioning as automatic good  
- Victory without personal ruin  

If any appear, *The Dark Knight* collapses into conventional superhero myth.

---

## Operational Outcome

Because these invariants are enforced:

- The antagonist feels philosophical, not theatrical  
- The hero becomes tragic rather than aspirational  
- Institutions appear brittle and human  
- Hope is preserved through lie and loss  
- The ending refuses moral comfort  

---

## Summary

*The Dark Knight* maintains coherence by enforcing grim constraints:

- systems fail first  
- purity collapses under pressure  
- chaos reveals truth  
- victory demands sacrifice  

The film works because **heroism is treated as an ethical wound, not a reward**.

**If doing the right thing felt good, *The Dark Knight* would be dishonest.**
