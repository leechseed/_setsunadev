# 📛 In Medias Res – Instant Action in Your RPGs

_In medias res_ means beginning in the middle of events rather than at the chronological start.

It eliminates warm-up exposition.  
It replaces setup with consequence.

Instead of explaining the world first, the technique forces players to **act first** and understand later.

This entry formalizes the technique structurally and explains how to deploy it without sacrificing clarity.

## The Middle of Things

_In medias res_ opens on a pivotal moment:

- A battle already underway.
    
- A ritual seconds from completion.
    
- A collapsing bridge mid-escape.
    
- A betrayal already exposed.
    

There is no slow introduction. Stakes are active from the first sentence.

### Immediate Engagement

This technique:

- Forces reaction.
    
- Creates urgency.
    
- Triggers curiosity.
    

Players ask:  
_What is happening?_  
_Why are we here?_  
_Who caused this?_

Confusion is not a flaw. It is the hook.

### Application

Begin a session:

- In the middle of a heist gone wrong.
    
- Surrounded by enemies.
    
- Already captured.
    
- Already blamed.
    

Action first. Context later.

## Organic Backstory Revelation

After the explosive opening, context must emerge gradually.

Backstory is revealed through:

- Dialogue.
    
- Flashbacks.
    
- Environmental clues.
    
- Found documents.
    
- Interrogations.
    
- Ruins or symbolic artifacts.
    

### Avoid Info Dumping

Exposition should never pause momentum.

Instead of narrating history:

- Let a wounded cultist reveal motive.
    
- Let a recovered data shard hint at conspiracy.
    
- Let a flashback trigger under stress.
    

Revelation should feel earned, not delivered.

### Movement Application (Bourne Phase)

In Movement 4:

- Memory fragments surface under pressure.
    
- Recognition occurs mid-conflict.
    
- Identity is reconstructed through action, not lectures.
    

The past is not explained. It intrudes.

## Crafting Intrigue

The opening scene functions as a **Mystery Box**.

Its purpose is to provoke:

> "How did we get here?"

A strong _in medias res_ opening must:

- Contain visible stakes.
    
- Suggest unseen history.
    
- Present unanswered questions.
    

Mystery without tension is weak.  
Tension without mystery is shallow.

You need both.

### Laban Flow Application

This technique shifts narrative tempo:

- From **Sustained** pacing
    
- To **Sudden** intensity
    

Sudden flow demands immediate decision-making.  
It prevents passive consumption.

---

## Structural Benefits

Properly executed, _in medias res_:

- Eliminates sluggish session openings.
    
- Establishes tone immediately.
    
- Forces player investment.
    
- Prioritizes action over exposition.
    
- Encourages retroactive meaning-making.
    

Players become investigators of their own circumstances.

---

## Design Execution Checklist

Before using _in medias res_, confirm:

- The opening scene contains clear stakes.
    
- There is a logical chain explaining how events occurred.
    
- Backstory clues are seeded intentionally.
    
- Players are not overwhelmed beyond comprehension.
    
- Resolution pathways exist beyond immediate survival.
    

Chaos must be structured underneath.

---

## Core Takeaway

_In medias res_ is not random action.  
It is controlled narrative shock.

Start in consequence.  
Reveal causation gradually.  
Let curiosity drive engagement.

For Movement 4 (Bourne), the Asset must awaken mid-pursuit—confused, hunted, and incomplete.

Understanding should not precede crisis.  
It should emerge from surviving it.
