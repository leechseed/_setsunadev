# Feeling is the Secret — Summary


 **Title** : Feeling is the Secret

 **Author** : Neville Goddard

 **Material Type** : Metaphysical / New Thought Monograph

 **Page Count** : ~50 pages (varies by edition)

 **First Published** : 1944
---

## Abstract

_Feeling is the Secret_ is a concise metaphysical treatise in which Neville Goddard argues that human experience is entirely determined by the feeling-states held in the subconscious mind. The book posits that consciousness is the sole reality, that the waking conscious mind functions as the selector of experience while the subconscious mind functions as the executor, and that the bridge between intention and manifestation is not thought but feeling. Goddard draws on scriptural interpretation — particularly his allegorical reading of the Bible — to establish that "feeling" is the creative medium through which all states of being are impressed upon the subconscious and subsequently externalized as lived reality. The work serves as both a philosophical statement and a practical manual, providing a specific sleep-based technique for deliberately impressing desired states upon the subconscious.

---

## TL;DR

- Consciousness is the only reality; the entire visible world is a projection of internal states.
- The subconscious mind accepts whatever the conscious mind impresses upon it through feeling and faithfully reproduces it as external circumstance.
- Thinking alone does not create; feeling is the exclusive medium of creation and the operative mechanism by which ideas are accepted by the subconscious.
- The conscious mind is the "male" or selective principle; the subconscious is the "female" or formative principle — together they generate all experience.
- Sleep is the most powerful entry point for impressing the subconscious because the conscious mind's rational defenses are relaxed.
- The prescribed technique is to assume the feeling of the wish fulfilled as you fall asleep, allowing that feeling to be the last impression carried into the subconscious.
- Prayer, in Goddard's framework, is not petition but the sustained assumption of already having what is desired — feeling as if the wish is already fact.
- The Bible is to be read as psychological allegory, not historical record; its figures and events represent states of consciousness.
- Every feeling that is sustained and believed — whether positive or negative — will eventually externalize as circumstance.
- The individual is never a victim of external forces; all causation originates within consciousness.
- To change one's outer world, one must first change one's inner feeling-state; no external manipulation can substitute.
- Doubt, fear, and anxiety are themselves creative feelings that manifest unwanted conditions with the same reliability as faith and confidence manifest desired ones.

## Chapter 1 — Law and Its Operation

**Summary** : Goddard opens with his foundational premise: the relationship between the conscious and subconscious mind constitutes an inviolable law that governs all manifestation. The conscious mind is the realm of selection and impression; the subconscious is the realm of expression and formation. Whatever feeling-state the conscious mind sustains with conviction is accepted by the subconscious without judgment and reproduced as external reality. This is not a metaphor — Goddard presents it as a literal operative law, functioning with the same impersonal regularity as gravity.

- **Consciousness as Sole Cause** : All phenomena originate in consciousness. The external world is an effect, never a cause. There is no power outside the individual's own awareness that determines experience.
- Events, circumstances, and conditions are projections of the feeling-states held internally.
- To seek change externally without altering the internal state is futile by definition.
- **The Conscious / Subconscious Dyad** : The conscious mind is the selector; it chooses what to focus on and what feeling to sustain. The subconscious mind is the builder; it receives impressions and externalizes them without discrimination.
- The subconscious does not evaluate truth or falsehood — it accepts whatever is impressed upon it with feeling.
- This means the subconscious will faithfully produce both desired and undesired outcomes depending on what feelings are dominant.
- **Feeling as the Medium** : Ideas alone are inert. An idea becomes causally active only when it is accompanied by feeling. The feeling is what converts a thought into an impression the subconscious will act upon.
- Intellectually affirming something without feeling produces no result.
- Conversely, a feeling sustained without intellectual elaboration is fully sufficient to produce results.
- **The Masculine / Feminine Metaphor** : Goddard characterizes the conscious mind as the masculine principle (initiating, selecting, impressing) and the subconscious as the feminine principle (receiving, gestating, forming). Their union — an idea felt as real — is the creative act that produces experience.
- This metaphor maps to numerous scriptural and mythological creation narratives, which Goddard reads as allegories for this psychological process.

  **Key Idea** : The law of consciousness operates impersonally — whatever feeling is impressed upon the subconscious with sustained conviction will externalize as reality, regardless of whether the individual intended that outcome or not.

## Chapter 2 — Sleep

**Summary** : Goddard identifies the transition into sleep as the most critical moment for deliberate creation. As the conscious mind relaxes its grip on sensory data and rational filtering, the subconscious becomes maximally receptive to impression. The feeling carried into sleep functions as a directive to the subconscious, which then works throughout the night to organize circumstances that correspond to that feeling. Goddard provides his core practical technique here: assume the feeling of the wish fulfilled and let that assumption be the last conscious impression before sleep.

- **Sleep as the Gateway** : The moments before sleep constitute a unique state of consciousness in which the boundary between conscious and subconscious is at its thinnest. Impressions made during this interval penetrate the subconscious with minimal resistance.
- The waking state is dominated by sensory input and rational evaluation, both of which can contradict the desired feeling and prevent its acceptance.
- In the drowsy pre-sleep state (sometimes called the "state akin to sleep" in Goddard's other works), these defenses are lowered.
- **The Technique** : As you fall asleep, construct a brief mental scene that implies the fulfillment of your desire. Do not visualize the process of attainment — visualize the end result as already accomplished. Most importantly, feel the reality of that scene.
- The scene should be vivid enough to evoke feeling but simple enough to sustain while drifting off.
- The goal is not to think about the desire but to think and feel from the state of having it fulfilled.
- Repeat the scene as a loop, allowing it to carry you into sleep.
- **Negative Sleep Impressions** : The same law applies in reverse. Falling asleep in states of worry, fear, resentment, or anxiety impresses those feelings upon the subconscious, which will then faithfully externalize corresponding conditions.
- Goddard treats this as one of the primary mechanisms by which people perpetuate unwanted life circumstances.
- Habitual negative feeling-states before sleep produce chronic negative conditions.
- **The Subconscious Works While You Sleep** : Once an impression is accepted, the subconscious organizes the means of its fulfillment without conscious intervention. The individual does not need to know how the desired state will come about — only that it will.
- This is the basis of Goddard's insistence that one should never concern oneself with the "how."

  **Key Idea** : The feeling held at the threshold of sleep is the most potent creative act available to the individual, because it bypasses the conscious mind's resistance and delivers its content directly to the subconscious for execution.

## Chapter 3 — Prayer

**Summary** : Goddard redefines prayer entirely, stripping it of its conventional religious framing as petition or supplication to an external deity. In his framework, prayer is the act of assuming the feeling of the wish fulfilled — not asking for something, but psychologically inhabiting the state in which it is already possessed. Successful prayer is therefore indistinguishable from successful feeling. Failed prayer — repetitive begging or anxious hoping — fails precisely because it sustains the feeling of lack rather than the feeling of fulfillment.

- **Prayer as Feeling, Not Petition** : To pray effectively is to feel the reality of the desired state. There is no external being to petition. The "God" of scripture, in Goddard's reading, is the individual's own deeper consciousness (the subconscious / I AM).
- Traditional petition-style prayer reinforces the consciousness of not having, which is the opposite of the creative state.
- Effective prayer requires the individual to psychologically shift from "wanting" to "having."
- **The State of Already Having** : The critical distinction is between desiring something and feeling that it is already accomplished. Desire accompanied by the feeling of absence creates more absence. Desire accompanied by the feeling of fulfillment creates fulfillment.
- This is the meaning Goddard assigns to the scriptural instruction to believe that you have received what you ask for.
- **Sustained Assumption** : A single fleeting feeling is insufficient. The feeling of the wish fulfilled must be sustained, returned to, and lived in until it becomes the dominant mood. Eventually, it hardens into fact.
- Doubt and contradiction between prayer sessions do not necessarily negate the impression, but they slow or prevent its acceptance.
- The ideal is to reach a point of inner conviction — a settled sense that it is done — after which the desire naturally drops away.

  **Key Idea** : Prayer is not communication with an external power but the internal act of assuming and sustaining the feeling-state of the wish fulfilled until the subconscious accepts it as fact and manifests it externally.

## Chapter 4 — Spirit – Feeling

**Summary** : In the final chapter, Goddard draws his metaphysical claims to their ultimate conclusion: spirit, feeling, and the subconscious are identified as aspects of a single creative principle. The Bible, read as psychological allegory, maps entirely onto Goddard's system. Every figure, event, and instruction in scripture describes a state of consciousness and the laws by which consciousness creates. The individual who understands this becomes a conscious creator, deliberately selecting and sustaining the feeling-states that correspond to desired experience.

- **Spirit = Feeling** : Goddard collapses the distinction between "spirit" and "feeling." When scripture speaks of the Spirit of God moving upon the waters, it speaks of feeling impressing itself upon the formless subconscious. Spirit is not an abstraction — it is the felt quality of a state of consciousness.
- To be "moved by the spirit" is to have one's feeling-state shifted.
- To be "filled with the spirit" is to have a feeling-state so completely assumed that it saturates one's entire consciousness.
- **Biblical Allegory** : Goddard reads scriptural narratives as encoded instruction manuals for the operation of consciousness. Adam and Eve, the Virgin Birth, the Crucifixion, and the Resurrection are all psychological events occurring within the individual.
- Creation stories describe the process by which consciousness (the "word" or idea) takes on form (flesh) through the medium of feeling.
- Miracles are demonstrations of the law of assumption carried to its logical conclusion.
- **The I AM** : The name of God — "I AM" — is the individual's own sense of being. Whatever follows "I AM" becomes the creative directive. "I am wealthy," felt as true, impresses wealth. "I am ill," felt as true, impresses illness.
- Goddard warns against carelessly appending negative states to "I AM" because the law operates without moral preference.
- **Total Responsibility** : If consciousness is the sole cause, the individual bears total responsibility for the content of their experience. There are no external forces, no luck, no fate operating independently of one's sustained feeling-states.
- This is not presented as blame but as empowerment — the same law that created unwanted conditions can be deliberately used to create desired ones.

  **Key Idea** : Spirit, feeling, and the subconscious creative principle are one and the same — the individual who masters the deliberate assumption of feeling-states masters the process by which all experience is generated.

## Key Concepts & Definitions

- **Consciousness** : The totality of an individual's awareness; the only reality and the sole cause of all external phenomena.
- **Subconscious Mind** : The receptive, formative dimension of consciousness that accepts impressions without judgment and externalizes them as circumstance.
- **Conscious Mind** : The selective, directive dimension of consciousness that chooses what ideas and feelings to focus on and impress upon the subconscious.
- **Feeling** : The operative medium of creation; the quality of emotional conviction that converts an idea into a causally active impression accepted by the subconscious.
- **The Law** : The impersonal mechanism by which feeling-states impressed upon the subconscious are faithfully reproduced as external experience.
- **Assumption** : The psychological act of inhabiting a desired state as though it were already real, sustained through feeling rather than intellectual effort.
- **The Wish Fulfilled** : The end state of a desire experienced in imagination as already accomplished — the target feeling-state for creative work.
- **State Akin to Sleep** : The drowsy, hypnagogic threshold between waking and sleeping in which the subconscious is maximally receptive to impression.
- **I AM** : The fundamental sense of being and existence; the name of God in Goddard's system, which functions as the root creative declaration from which all specific states branch.
- **Prayer** : The sustained assumption of the feeling of the wish fulfilled; not petition to an external being but an internal shift in consciousness.
- **Spirit** : Identified with feeling itself; the animating creative force that impresses form upon the formless subconscious.
- **Masculine / Feminine Principle** : Goddard's metaphor for the conscious mind (masculine, initiating, impressing) and the subconscious mind (feminine, receiving, forming).
- **Externalization** : The process by which an internally sustained feeling-state manifests as visible, tangible circumstance in the outer world.
- **Impression** : The act of conveying a feeling-charged idea to the subconscious, which then treats it as a blueprint for manifestation.

## Structural / Thematic Synthesis

The architecture of _Feeling is the Secret_ is deliberately minimal and cumulative. Each of its four chapters restates and deepens a single thesis: feeling is the sole creative agent, and all experience is its product. Chapter 1 establishes the law and the mechanism. Chapter 2 provides the practical technique keyed to the sleep state. Chapter 3 reframes an existing cultural practice (prayer) through the lens of the law. Chapter 4 elevates the thesis to a complete metaphysical worldview by identifying feeling with spirit and reading all of scripture as encrypted documentation of this same process.

The overarching argument moves from mechanics to practice to philosophy. Goddard never introduces competing models, qualifications, or exceptions — the entire work is a single sustained assertion delivered with increasing scope. This structural simplicity is itself rhetorical: by refusing to acknowledge alternatives or complications, Goddard positions the law as self-evident and absolute.

Several cross-cutting patterns recur throughout. The inversion of conventional causality — inner states cause outer conditions, never the reverse — is restated in every chapter from a different angle. The insistence on feeling over thought is similarly omnipresent, functioning as the book's central corrective against intellectualized or willpower-based approaches to change. The sleep technique and the redefinition of prayer both serve as practical applications of the same underlying principle, differing only in context (private nightly practice vs. ongoing conscious assumption throughout the day).

Goddard's hermeneutic method — reading the Bible as psychological allegory — is not merely supplementary but structurally essential. It provides the authority structure for his claims, positions his teaching within a tradition (however heterodox), and offers the reader a reinterpretive framework that transforms familiar material into evidence for the law. Whether one accepts this hermeneutic or not, recognizing its structural role is necessary for understanding why Goddard's system presents itself as discovery rather than invention.

## Neville Goddard's Key Contributions

### Contribution 1: **Goddard's Feeling-Primacy Thesis**

- **Title** : Goddard's Feeling-Primacy Thesis
- **Type** : Proposition
- **Core Claim** : Feeling, not thought, visualization, willpower, or action, is the exclusive medium by which consciousness creates experience. An idea without feeling is inert; a feeling without elaborate ideation is fully causally operative.
- **Components** :
- Distinction between intellectual assent and felt conviction
- Identification of feeling as the "language" the subconscious understands
- Dismissal of all non-feeling-based methods as ineffective
- **Application** : A practitioner tests whether a desired state has been genuinely assumed by checking the quality of feeling, not the clarity of thought or the intensity of effort.
- **Cross-References** : Comparable to [[William James]]'s "as-if" principle; contrasts with [[Norman Vincent Peale]]'s emphasis on positive thinking; parallels [[Joseph Murphy]]'s subconscious impression model but with stronger emphasis on feeling over affirmation.

---

### Contribution 2: **The Sleep Impression Technique**

- **Title** : Goddard's Sleep Impression Technique (State Akin to Sleep)
- **Type** : Heuristic / Technique
- **Core Claim** : The hypnagogic state immediately preceding sleep is the optimal window for impressing the subconscious, because the conscious mind's critical faculty is relaxed and the subconscious is maximally receptive.
- **Components** :
- Identification of the pre-sleep state as a discrete operational window
- Construction of a short, looping mental scene implying wish fulfillment
- Emphasis on feeling from the scene rather than merely seeing it
- Use of repetition to carry the feeling into sleep
- **Application** : The practitioner selects a single desire, constructs a brief scene that would naturally follow its fulfillment, and replays it with feeling nightly until inner conviction is achieved.
- **Cross-References** : Parallels [[hypnagogic suggestion]] in clinical hypnotherapy; related to [[autosuggestion]] as developed by [[Émile Coué]]; anticipates elements of modern [[SATS]] practice in Neville Goddard study communities.

---

### Contribution 3: **Prayer as Assumption (Anti-Petition Model)**

- **Title** : Goddard's Anti-Petition Prayer Model
- **Type** : Framework
- **Core Claim** : Effective prayer is not petition directed toward an external deity but the sustained internal assumption of already possessing what is desired. Petition-style prayer reinforces the consciousness of lack and is therefore self-defeating.
- **Components** :
- Redefinition of prayer from request to state-shift
- Identification of "God" with the individual's own subconscious / I AM
- Criterion for successful prayer: the feeling of fulfillment, not the performance of ritual
- Failure mode: any prayer that sustains the feeling of wanting rather than having
- **Application** : Instead of asking for something, the practitioner enters the feeling-state of already having it, sustains that state, and allows the subconscious to produce corresponding external conditions.
- **Cross-References** : Resonates with [[Thomas Troward]]'s "mental science" approach to prayer; contrasts with orthodox Christian petition models; parallels [[Florence Scovel Shinn]]'s affirmation-based prayer but privileges feeling over spoken word.

---

### Contribution 4: **The I AM Ontology**

- **Title** : Goddard's I AM Ontology
- **Type** : Framework / Proposition
- **Core Claim** : The biblical name of God — "I AM" — refers to the individual's own fundamental awareness of being. Whatever state is appended to "I AM" and sustained with feeling becomes the operative creative directive that the subconscious externalizes.
- **Components** :
- Identification of "I AM" as pure unconditioned awareness
- Any state following "I AM" functions as a self-definition the subconscious enacts
- The law operates identically for positive and negative self-definitions
- All scriptural references to God's name encode this principle
- **Application** : The practitioner monitors and deliberately selects the states they attach to their sense of "I AM," recognizing that habitual self-definitions (I am poor, I am sick, I am unworthy) are active creative directives, not neutral descriptions.
- **Cross-References** : Draws from [[Hermetic]] and [[Kabbalistic]] traditions of the divine name; parallels [[Ernest Holmes]]'s "Science of Mind" treatment of the I AM; connects to [[Abdullah]]'s reported oral teachings as transmitted by Goddard.
