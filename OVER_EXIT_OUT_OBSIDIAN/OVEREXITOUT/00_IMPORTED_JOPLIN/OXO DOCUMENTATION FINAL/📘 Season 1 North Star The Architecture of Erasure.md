## 1. The Season Claim

**"The system only protects what it can categorize."**

In Season 1, power is not exercised through violence, but through **classification**. To be "safe" is to be legible to the system's sensors. To be "erased" is to have one's category revoked.

The season is a demonstration of how the **Ecclesial** and **Guild** apparatuses maintain order by narrowing the definition of a "person" until the protagonists no longer fit the criteria.

## 3. The Primary Interface: The Feed

**The Feed** is the narrative's manifestation of the **Ecclesial Apparatus (Grace)**.

- It is the "Social Record" that confers legitimacy.
    
- **Verification** is the modern sacrament.
    
- **Banning** is the modern excommunication.
    
- **The Algorithm** is the mechanical law that dictates who is "seen" and who is "invisible."

## 4. Structural Mechanics of the 'Ban'

The "Ban" is the primary weapon used against Victoria and Riley. It functions as a **Terminal State** at the social scale.

- **Non-Physical Containment**: The system does not need to imprison the body if it can disconnect the body from the infrastructure of survival (banking, transport, communication).
    
- **Identity as a Credential**: Identity is not something a character _has_; it is a credential the system _issues_. When the credential is "Banned," the character's lived truth (what happened on the track) is rendered systemically irrelevant.
    

---

## 5. The 'Noise' Constraint

In this IP, characters who are "Banned" become **Noise**.

- **Noise** is information that the system is programmed to ignore or filter out.
    
- When Victoria tries to speak the truth, it is processed by the public (The Feed) not as "Evidence," but as "Spam" or "Glitch."
    
- The tragedy of Season 1 is the protagonist's realization that **being right doesn't matter if you are filtered.**

2. **The Heroic Recognition Veto**: Strangers cannot recognize Victoria from "The Feed" once she is banned. If she isn't in the signal, she doesn't exist in the public's consciousness.
    
3. **The Reset Veto**: No character can "start over" with a clean slate. Once you are "Noise," the systemâ€™s "Archive" remembers the error, even if it ignores the person.
