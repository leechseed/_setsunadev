## 1. Definition of an Outlier

In statistics, an **Outlier** is a data point that differs significantly from other observations.

- **The System's View**: An outlier is an error, noise, or a glitch that must be "smoothed over" or "removed" to maintain the integrity of the average (The Feed).
    
- **The Individual's View**: The outlier is the only part of the data that represents true change, genius, or catastrophe.
    

## 2. Dual Narrative Significance

The title functions across both of the series' primary timelines:

### The Racing Timeline (Past)

In the world of high-stakes racing and elite schooling, being an "Outlier" is a badge of **Superiority**. Victoria strives to be the outlier in terms of speed and skill. However, she discovers that the system only rewards outliers it can control. If her performance (Equity) challenges the Administration's plan, she is re-categorized as "Noise."

## 3. The Systematic Argument

The title perfectly encapsulates the **Dramatica Problem (Equity)** and the **Layer 5 Invariant (Legibility)**:

- **The Conflict**: The Administration wants a predictable, legible world.
    
- **The Problem**: Human experience (Grief, Truth, Memory) is messy and unpredictable.
    
- **The Result**: Anyone who insists on the **Truth** (Victoria's Flaw) automatically becomes an Outlier.
