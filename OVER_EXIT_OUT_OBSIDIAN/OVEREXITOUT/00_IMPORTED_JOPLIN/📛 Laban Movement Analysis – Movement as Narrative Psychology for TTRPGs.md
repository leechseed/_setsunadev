# 📛 Laban Movement Analysis – Movement as Narrative Psychology for TTRPGs

Laban Movement Analysis (LMA) treats movement as a language of energy, weight, and intention.

For tabletop roleplaying games, this shifts focus away from **what** a creature does (damage, actions, abilities) and toward **how** it moves. Movement becomes a carrier of emotion, psychology, and threat.

Combat stops feeling like math.  
It starts feeling like physics with intent.

## The Core Philosophy

LMA assumes:

- Movement communicates intention.
    
- The human nervous system reads energy instantly.
    
- Players respond emotionally to motion before they process mechanics.
    

In TTRPG design, this means:

- A monster’s gait can signal inevitability.
    
- A villain’s pacing can signal predation.
    
- A kingdom’s soldiers can signal cultural discipline through uniform motion.
    

Movement becomes psychological exposition without dialogue.

---

## The Four Dimensions of Movement

All physical action can be mapped across four primary sliders.

### Weight

- **Light** — delicate, evasive, brittle
    
- **Heavy** — crushing, immovable, inevitable
    

Weight signals threat mass and authority.

---

### Time

- **Sudden** — explosive, flickering, sharp
    
- **Sustained** — grinding, measured, patient
    

Time signals volatility versus inevitability.

---

### Space

- **Direct** — head-on, confrontational
    
- **Indirect** — circling, flanking, misleading
    

Space signals honesty versus predation.

---

### Flow

- **Bound** — restrained, rigid, controlled
    
- **Free** — wild, spinning, momentum-driven
    

Flow signals discipline versus abandon.

---

## The Laban Combat Matrix

Before an encounter, assign a **Movement Identity** by selecting one quality from each dimension.

This prevents encounters from collapsing into generic HP exchanges.

### Examples

**The Inevitable – Skeleton**  
Light / Sustained / Direct / Bound

- Moves with eerie patience
    
- Does not rush
    
- Simply continues forward
    

---

**The Force of Nature – Dragon**  
Heavy / Sustained / Direct / Free

- Displaces air
    
- Moves like collapsing architecture
    
- Momentum dominates space
    

---

**The Predator – Wraith**  
Light / Sudden / Indirect / Free

- Appears from blind angles
    
- Never stands still
    
- Feels impossible to pin down
    

Movement identity creates consistency across turns.

## Effort States – The Eight Emotional Signatures

Effort states combine Weight, Time, and Space into distinct moods. These work in combat, politics, horror, and social tension.

### Press (Strong / Sustained / Direct)

Relentless determination.  
Sieges, interrogations, imperial tightening.

---

### Flick (Light / Sudden / Indirect)

Playful or nervous intelligence.  
Fae mischief, ambush predators.

---

### Slash (Strong / Sudden / Indirect)

Violent rupture.  
Berserkers, sudden betrayal, collapsing order.

---

### Punch (Strong / Sudden / Direct)

Brutal authority.  
Executions, shock assaults, decisive violence.

---

### Glide (Light / Sustained / Direct)

Smooth control.  
Court politics, elite assassins, quiet mastery.

---

### Float (Light / Sustained / Indirect)

Eerie serenity.  
Spirits, prophecy, altered states.

---

### Dab (Light / Sudden / Direct)

Surgical precision.  
Scouts, trap triggers, rapid reactions.

---

### Wring (Strong / Sustained / Indirect)

Tormented pressure.  
Body horror, moral suffocation, slow corruption.

Each faction can be assigned an effort signature for thematic cohesion.

## The Kinosphere – The Bubble of Presence

The **Kinosphere** is the radius of influence an entity exerts without moving.

It is narrative pressure before mechanics engage.

### Small Kinosphere

- Threat feels local
    
- Anxiety only when adjacent
    

### Massive Kinosphere

- Air warms
    
- Floors vibrate
    
- Shadows distort before arrival
    

### Social Kinosphere

- A lich claims a room without raising its voice
    
- A king silences a hall through posture alone
    

Kinosphere telegraphs danger fairly and builds tension before initiative.

---

## Shape Flow – Power Over Time

Shape Flow describes how energy moves through a system — magical, political, emotional.

### Condensing / Sinking

- Gravity increases
    
- Breath shortens
    
- Life pulls inward
    

Common in necromancy, authoritarian power, despair.

---

### Rising / Spreading

- Air lightens
    
- Dust lifts
    
- Fear loosens
    

Common in divine intervention, hope, revolutionary momentum.

Shape Flow allows world-building through environmental reaction.

## Inner Attitude vs Outer Motion

Tension intensifies when **Inner Intent** conflicts with **Outer Motion**.

### The Mismatch Principle

Example:

- Merchant smiles warmly (Outer Glide)
    
- Moves with pressing, constricting energy (Inner Press)
    

Players sense the trap before it is spoken.

Mismatch creates unease, suspicion, and psychological depth without exposition.

---

## Design Principle Summary

LMA reframes combat and encounter design as physics-driven storytelling.

When you alter movement identity:

- You alter perceived danger.
    
- You alter emotional tone.
    
- You alter psychological clarity.
    

Movement becomes a conversation.

The table stops reacting to numbers.

They start reacting to energy.

That is the shift.
