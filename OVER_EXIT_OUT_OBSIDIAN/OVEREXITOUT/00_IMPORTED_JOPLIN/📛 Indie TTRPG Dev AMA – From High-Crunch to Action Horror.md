# 📛 Indie TTRPG Dev AMA – From High-Crunch to Action Horror

This discussion traces a designer’s shift from modifying an existing high-crunch system into building a distinct action horror identity. The turning point was not a minor rules tweak — it was a conceptual reframing of what the game was _about_.

The evolution demonstrates how mechanical focus determines genre tone, and how removing legacy assumptions (like Hit Points) can fundamentally redefine a system’s identity.

## The Injury System Eureka

The designer describes a critical breakthrough: the game’s identity only “clicked” after abandoning traditional Hit Points in favor of a visceral injury system.

Before this shift:

- The project resembled a modified high-crunch cyberpunk game.
    
- Damage functioned as abstract depletion.
    
- Combat followed familiar attrition patterns.
    

After replacing HP with injuries:

- Damage became specific and physical.
    
- The body became the central locus of tension.
    
- Combat stopped being numerical subtraction and became survival under trauma.
    

The transition reframed the game from:

Stat optimization under risk  
to  
Embodied horror under pressure.

Injury systems emphasize:

- Consequence permanence.
    
- Escalating physical limitation.
    
- Narrative realism.
    
- Psychological weight.
    

Each wound changes capability.  
Each encounter carries cumulative cost.

The identity shift was not cosmetic. It was structural.

The system moved from “numbers-first” to “body-first.”

## Classless Social Mechanics

Another key decision involved resisting the urge to over-mechanize social interaction.

In genres where action and survival dominate:

- Deep social subsystem complexity may dilute focus.
    
- Extended negotiation mechanics may slow pacing.
    

Instead, the designer implemented a streamlined “Grit Defense” system for social resolution.

This approach:

- Resolves social pressure quickly.
    
- Avoids elaborate dialogue trees.
    
- Maintains narrative pace.
    

The guiding principle is proportional design:

If the genre focus is action horror,  
the system should prioritize physical tension over rhetorical simulation.

Not every pillar requires equal mechanical depth.

Mechanical investment should mirror genre emphasis.

## Laban Application The Meat Focus

The injury system can be analyzed through movement dynamics.

### Weight

The system embraces heaviness.

- Bodies break.
    
- Exhaustion accumulates.
    
- Injury carries gravity.
    

Damage is not light abstraction.  
It is heavy and immediate.

---

### Flow

The transition from ordinary person to hardened operative often occurs through a sudden rupture.

In Laban terms:

- Sudden shift in identity.
    
- Bound flow under discipline.
    
- Heavy consequences under pressure.
    

The character’s narrative motion changes permanently.

The system reinforces that shift physically.

---

### Time

Combat pacing in injury-focused systems often feels:

- Sharp.
    
- High risk.
    
- Decisive.
    

Because even small mistakes can escalate into long-term impairment.

Time becomes compressed.

Moments matter.

## Design Identity Shift

The AMA illustrates a broader principle:

A system finds its identity when mechanics reinforce genre.

The pivot from high-crunch cyberpunk math to injury-driven action horror demonstrates:

- Mechanics are thematic statements.
    
- Removing inherited conventions clarifies tone.
    
- Physical vulnerability increases tension.
    

When a designer aligns mechanical focus with experiential promise, the system stops feeling derivative.

It becomes specific.

Injury systems emphasize:

- Embodiment.
    
- Vulnerability.
    
- Escalating consequence.
    
- Psychological intensity.
    

The result is not just a different combat engine.

It is a different worldview encoded in rules.

When the body becomes central, horror becomes tangible.
