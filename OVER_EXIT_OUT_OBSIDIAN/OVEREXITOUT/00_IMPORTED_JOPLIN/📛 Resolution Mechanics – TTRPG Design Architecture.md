# 📛 Resolution Mechanics – TTRPG Design Architecture

Resolution mechanics are the procedures that convert uncertain fiction into concrete outcomes. They are not neutral systems of math — they encode a game’s emotional stance, pacing logic, and thematic priorities.

Mechanics are design arguments.

## Theoretical Frameworks

Resolution systems shape behavior at the table before anyone consciously notices.

### MDA Framework

**Mechanics → Dynamics → Aesthetics**

- **Mechanics** create rules.
    
- **Dynamics** emerge from how players interact with those rules.
    
- **Aesthetics** are the emotional outcomes (tension, triumph, dread, etc.).
    

Change the mechanic, and you change the emotion.

---

### GNS Theory

Resolution mechanics often reveal which priority dominates the design:

- **Gameism** — challenge, fairness, tactical mastery
    
- **Narrativism** — theme, character consequence, dramatic structure
    
- **Simulationism** — modeling causality and internal realism
    

The resolution engine signals what the game believes is important.

---

### DDE Model

Resolution rules influence:

- **Decisions** — what choices feel meaningful
    
- **Dynamism** — how volatile or stable outcomes are
    
- **Discovery** — what players learn about the system and world
    
- **Expression** — how players differentiate themselves
    

A flat d20 system produces very different DDE outcomes than a dice pool or banded system.

## Timing of Resolution – Fortune

When you roll matters as much as what you roll.

### Fortune in the Middle

Dice are rolled before narration is finalized.

The fiction must bend to fit the result.

- Promotes emergent storytelling
    
- Encourages improvisation
    
- Reinforces consequence-driven drama
    

---

### Fortune at the End

Players declare a fully formed action first.

The dice confirm or deny it.

- Reinforces intent clarity
    
- Feels more task-oriented
    
- Emphasizes execution over reinterpretation
    

Timing determines whether fiction adapts to mechanics or mechanics validate fiction.

## Common Resolution Families

|Family|Mechanism|Pros|Cons|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Flat (d20)**|1 die + modifiers|Fast, intuitive, swingy drama|Bonus compression; experts fail often|
|**Bell Curve**|3d6 / Percentile|Predictable competence; realism|Fewer spectacular outliers; math weight|
|**Dice Pools**|Count hits or highest|Tactile; supports degrees of success|Table clutter; fuzzy probability intuition|
|**Banded**|2d6 result tiers|Fail Forward baked in; drama-first|Lower tactical granularity; GM load|
|**Symbolic**|Custom icons|Multi-dimensional results|Proprietary dice; interpretation burden|
|**Physical**|Jenga / dex tasks|Embodied tension; strong for horror|Accessibility limits; genre constrained|

Each family encodes a different philosophy of uncertainty.

## The Design Levers

Designers tune a system’s “feel” using these primary sliders.

### 1. Probability Shape

- **Flat** — surprising, heroic, chaotic
    
- **Bell** — predictable, gritty, competence-forward
    

This controls how often expertise matters.

---

### 2. Outcome Granularity

- **Binary** — Yes / No
    
- **Degrees** — Yes and… / Yes but… / No but…
    

Granularity determines whether failure stalls or propels story.

---

### 3. Fiction Integration

- **Numeric modifiers** — mechanical precision
    
- **Narrative triggers** — fiction directly modifies mechanics (Position, Effect, Tags)
    

Integration controls how tightly narrative logic binds to resolution.

---

### 4. Consequence Visibility

- **Hidden stakes** — suspense, opacity
    
- **Tracked pressure** — clocks, stress tracks, escalation meters
    

Visibility shapes agency and tension.

## Common Design Pitfalls and Fixes

### Binary Stallouts

A failed roll halts narrative momentum.

**Fix:**

- Use **Fail Forward**
    
- Introduce **Mixed Success**
    
- Attach consequences instead of denial
    

---

### Over-Swingy Dice

High variance undermines expertise.

**Fix:**

- Use bell curves
    
- Apply **Bounded Accuracy**
    
- Introduce competency floors
    

---

### Opaque Stakes

Players roll without understanding risk.

**Fix:**

- **Declare stakes before rolling**
    
- Frame consequences explicitly
    

---

### Roll Overload

Rolling for trivial actions dilutes tension.

**Fix:**

- Roll only when stakes are meaningful
    
- Use **Conflict Resolution** instead of task-by-task resolution
    

---

## Design Principle Summary

Resolution mechanics are not just probability engines.

They determine:

- What kind of risk feels fair
    
- What kind of competence feels earned
    
- Whether story stalls or escalates
    
- Whether emotion emerges from structure
    

If the mechanic does not reinforce the intended emotional experience, the system will fight the story.

Align the math with the mood.

That is the real design work.
