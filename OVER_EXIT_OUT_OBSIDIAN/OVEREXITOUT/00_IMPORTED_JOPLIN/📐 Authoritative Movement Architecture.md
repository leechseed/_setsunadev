# What a Movement Is

A **Movement** is a **macro-scale narrative phase** defined by a shift in:

- Systemic pressure  
- Genre expression  
- Characterâ€“system relationship  
- What kind of survival is possible  

A Movement is **not a plot arc**.  
It is a **change in the operating environment of the story**.

Each Movement changes:
- What the system wants  
- What the protagonist believes  
- What costs are unavoidable  
- What kind of failure becomes likely  

Movements are **ontological climate shifts**, not emotional beats.

---

# What a Movement Is NOT

A Movement is NOT:

- A character arc  
- A theme  
- A subplot  
- A setting change alone  
- A tonal experiment  

If the **rules of pressure** donâ€™t change, it is not a new Movement.
