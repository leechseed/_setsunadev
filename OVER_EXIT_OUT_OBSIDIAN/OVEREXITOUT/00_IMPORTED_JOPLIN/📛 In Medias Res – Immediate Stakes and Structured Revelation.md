# 📛 In Medias Res – Immediate Stakes and Structured Revelation

_In medias res_ is a narrative technique that begins in the middle of a critical moment rather than at chronological origin.

Instead of starting with exposition, background, or setup, the session opens with action already underway. The characters are already in danger, mid-heist, mid-battle, or mid-decision.

This technique solves a common pacing issue in tabletop campaigns: slow openings that delay engagement while context is explained.

By beginning with immediate stakes, you:

- Force player reaction
    
- Generate curiosity
    
- Establish tone instantly
    
- Create forward momentum
    

The method does not remove structure. It rearranges it.

## The Middle of Things

Beginning in the middle means opening on a moment of instability.

Examples:

- The party is already mid-heist, alarms blaring.
    
- A ritual is half-complete and something is emerging.
    
- The bridge is collapsing and initiative is already rolling.
    
- The characters wake up captured.
    

The key is immediacy.

Players are placed into a reactive state rather than a passive listening state.

### Reactive Mindset

Immediate action produces:

- Heightened attention
    
- Tactical thinking
    
- Emotional activation
    

Rather than asking, “What is happening?” in a detached way, players ask, “What do we do right now?”

This creates urgency.

### Application Strategy

To implement effectively:

- Start with a clear sensory scene.
    
- State the immediate danger or tension.
    
- Ask for action within the first minute.
    

Do not explain how they arrived there yet.

Let action come first.

## Organic Backstory Revelation

Once immediate tension stabilizes, context can be revealed gradually.

This prevents **information dumping**, which occurs when a large amount of exposition is delivered in a single block, often reducing engagement.

Instead, reveal background through:

- Dialogue
    
- Interrogation
    
- Environmental storytelling
    
- Flashbacks
    
- Physical evidence
    

Example structure:

Ambush occurs → Enemy survives → Interrogation reveals faction motive.

The revelation emerges from action, not lecture.

### Flashbacks as Controlled Inserts

Flashbacks should:

- Clarify stakes
    
- Deepen character history
    
- Reframe present action
    

They should not interrupt momentum without purpose.

In a memory-driven arc such as a “Bourne” phase, flashbacks can be triggered by:

- Familiar locations
    
- Recognized symbols
    
- Physical trauma
    

Backstory becomes a discovery process rather than an opening exposition.

## Crafting Intrigue

The opening scene should function as a mystery engine.

Players should immediately ask:

- How did we get here?
    
- Who caused this?
    
- What are we missing?
    

This is the “Mystery Box” principle.

You do not answer everything immediately. You establish uncertainty.

### Laban Flow – Sudden Activation

In pacing terms, this shifts from:

- Sustained Flow — slow buildup  
    to
    
- Sudden Flow — immediate pressure
    

Sudden flow requires:

- Clear stakes
    
- Immediate consequences
    
- Rapid decisions
    

The goal is not chaos for its own sake.

The goal is activated curiosity.

## Structural Control and Escalation

Beginning in the middle does not mean abandoning structure.

You must still:

- Know what led to this moment
    
- Understand faction motivations
    
- Track timeline causality
    

The difference is delivery order.

Chronology for players:

1. Crisis
    
2. Partial explanation
    
3. Investigation
    
4. Full revelation
    

Chronology for GM:

1. World setup
    
2. Cause
    
3. Escalation
    
4. Crisis
    

You rearrange presentation, not logic.

## Teaching Summary

In medias res:

- Begins with instability
    
- Forces immediate reaction
    
- Defers explanation
    
- Reveals context through action
    
- Sustains intrigue through unanswered questions
    

Avoid:

- Overloading exposition
    
- Confusing players with unclear stakes
    
- Opening with passive briefing
    

For Movement 4 (Bourne):

Start pursued.  
Start confused.  
Start already compromised.

Memory and identity should unfold under pressure.

---

## Core Takeaway

In medias res is a pacing tool.

It:

- Accelerates engagement
    
- Creates curiosity
    
- Prioritizes action over explanation
    
- Restructures narrative order without sacrificing coherence
    

Start in the fire.

Explain the smoke later.
